Overview

ICEVSG 2026 conference highlights

The International Conference on Electric Vehicles and Smart Grid brings together researchers, utilities, automakers, policymakers, and city leaders to align vehicle electrification with power-system resilience. Across three days of keynotes, technical sessions, tutorials, and networking, the conference will focus on smart charging, storage, grid flexibility, data-driven operations, and equitable infrastructure planning.

Conference tracks

Vehicle-to-grid integration

<p>Managed charging, bidirectional power flow, and control strategies that turn EV fleets into grid assets.</p>

Smart charging and interoperability

<p>Standards, protocols, and utility coordination that make public and private charging reliable at scale.</p>

Storage and distribution planning

<p>Battery storage, feeder constraints, and distribution modeling for rapid load growth.</p>

Power electronics and grid resilience

<p>Converters, protection, harmonics, and resilience engineering for electrified infrastructure.</p>

Data, AI, and digital twins

<p>Forecasting, optimization, anomaly detection, and operational analytics for transport-energy systems.</p>

Policy, markets, and equity

<p>Tariffs, incentives, congestion, access, and planning decisions that determine who benefits from electrification.</p>
